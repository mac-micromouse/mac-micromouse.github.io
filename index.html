About

Originally started by IEEE in the late 1970s, Micromouse is a robotics competition where tiny robots race through a maze completely on their own. Teams design, build, and program their robots to explore the maze and find the fastest route to the center as quick as possible.

McMaster Micromouse hosts workshops and help-sessions leading to a competition, allowing students to gain experience from embedded systems to pathfinding algorithms.

All levels of experience are welcomed!
